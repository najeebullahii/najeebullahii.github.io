Telco Customer Churn Prediction

Built and compared three machine learning models to predict which telecom customers are about to cancel their service — and why. XGBoost won with AUC-ROC of 0.84.

The Business Problem

Why Customers Leave and How to Keep Them

Customer churn costs telecom companies millions annually. Acquiring a new customer costs five to seven times more than retaining an existing one. This project analyses 7,043 customer records to identify which customers are at risk before they leave — and translates those findings into specific retention actions a business team can act on.

The current churn rate in this dataset is 26.54% — roughly one in four customers. Four patterns explain the majority of that behaviour.

Overall churn distribution — 26.54% of customers churned, which is high enough to justify a dedicated predictive effort.
Key Insights

What the Data Tells Us

Churn rates by contract type, internet service, and payment method — the three most predictive categorical variables.
The Contract Trap: Month-to-month customers churn at dramatically higher rates than those on one or two-year plans. The flexibility that attracts them is the same flexibility that makes it easy to leave.
The Fiber Optic Paradox: Fiber optic customers — the highest-value subscribers — churn more than DSL users. Something is broken in that experience, whether pricing, reliability, or service quality.
Payment Friction: Customers paying by electronic check churn more than those on automatic billing. Manual payment introduces friction and missed payments.
The First Year: Churn is heavily concentrated in the first 12 months. Customers who survive year one become dramatically more loyal.
Churn rate by tenure band — the first 12 months are the danger zone.
